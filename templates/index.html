Discovery Brief Agent

Trigger: HubSpot webhook (meeting booked)  ·  Model: Claude Opus 4.6  ·  lgoldfarb-hub/discovery-brief-agent

Automatically researches a prospect before a discovery call. When a new meeting is booked in HubSpot, this agent pulls deal and contact data, enriches it via Amplemarket, runs live web research, and generates a 16-section MEDDPICC-style brief saved directly as a HubSpot note on the deal. It also advances the deal stage to "First Meeting Scheduled" so the pipeline stays accurate. The goal is for the AE to walk into every call knowing the prospect's integration landscape, pain points, and ICP fit — without doing any manual research.

Rules
🚦 Qualification gate
Only runs for deals in the Global Embedded Pipeline at the "Lead or Opportunity (Meeting Booked)" stage. Any other pipeline or stage → agent exits early with no brief generated. This is the most common reason a brief silently doesn't appear.
🔍 Signal Research reuse
Before running web research for §9 (Trigger Signals), the agent checks whether a Signal Research Agent note already exists on the deal. If found, it reuses that content directly — skipping the web search step entirely. If no signal note exists, it runs run_signal_research() to generate one fresh.
📊 Deal stage advancement
After every successful brief, the deal stage is automatically advanced to "First Meeting Scheduled" (stage ID: 113001644). This cannot be disabled on the auto-trigger flow.

Rules
✅ COMPLETED
Sufficient evidence the intended action was fully executed. "Follow up with X" → any email, call, or note logged today. "Book a meeting" → meeting scheduled or logged. "Send proposal" → email logged or note confirms sent. Compound-intent tasks (e.g. "Follow up with X; schedule a call") require BOTH intents satisfied — an email alone is not enough if scheduling is required.
⚠️ INCOMPLETE
Some activity exists but doesn't fully meet the task intent — e.g. a note was added but no actual outreach was sent, or an email was sent but its content doesn't match the task intent.
❌ MISSED
No engagement of any kind logged today on the deal, and no next task exists to indicate follow-up was rescheduled.
❓ NOT ENOUGH DATA
Task title is too ambiguous to determine intent, deal association is missing, or the action likely happened outside HubSpot (e.g. WhatsApp, phone).
❌ DEALS WITH NO PLANNED TASKS
A separate section appended to each channel's report: all active deals in the tracked stages that have no open task with a future due date. Runs even on days with zero overdue tasks.
🚫 DEALS NOT WORTH ACTIVE CHASING
Identifies deals in Lead and Post-Meeting Qualification stages only that no longer warrant active follow-up. Claude assigns one of two suggestions per deal: Move to Closed Lost or Move to Contact Later. Report is grouped by stage (Lead / Post-Meeting). All other stages are excluded from this sweep.

Pre-filter exclusions: Lead deals with a future meeting booked are always excluded. Post-meeting deals with an active next step (future meeting, active POC, mutual action plan, procurement/legal review, or concrete next step agreed within 14 days) are excluded — Claude applies this check and sets action = "exclude" for those deals.

Closed Lost — High ICP (≥ 8): Lead lifetime ≥ 30d; or Post-Meeting ≥ 20d since last meeting; Block 3 (Engagement) ≤ 5; total follow-ups ≥ 5; follow-ups with signals ≥ 1; no meaningful progression in last 14 days.
Closed Lost — Lower ICP (< 8): Lead lifetime ≥ 14d; or Post-Meeting ≥ 20d since last meeting; Block 3 ≤ 6; total follow-ups ≥ 4; no active next step.

Contact Later — High ICP (≥ 8): Same thresholds as Closed Lost; Block 3 > 7; total follow-ups ≥ 5; signals ≥ 1; at least one of: prior interest shown, timing/bandwidth/budget objection, positive reply, strong ICP fit, future trigger.
Contact Later — Lower ICP (< 8): Lead ≥ 14d or Post-Meeting ≥ 20d since last meeting; Block 3 > 7; total follow-ups ≥ 4; positive signal or timing objection.

Buying Intent note lookup checks deal-associated notes first, then falls back to contact-associated notes (HubSpot Enrichment creates notes on the contact, not the deal). Block 3 score parsed via regex from note body (handles *Block 3 (Engagement)*: N / 25 format).
🚫 Excluded from evidence
Agent-generated notes are excluded (case-insensitive prefix match): any note starting with "buying intent / intent levels (ai)", "weekly review (ai)", "ai meeting evaluation", "auto-generated", or "reactivation review note:". Content of emails and notes is passed to Claude to verify the engagement actually matches the task intent — activity count alone is not sufficient for Completed.

Rule 2 — Advance to "2nd Meeting Booked"
Deal has 2+ meetings, the latest meeting is in Scheduled or Rescheduled status, and at least one prior meeting was completed → advance stage to "2nd Meeting Booked".
Rule 3 — Mark follow-up completed
Deal has 2+ meetings and all meetings are in Completed status → set status field to "Follow-up meeting completed".

Deal has exactly 1 meeting in Completed status → advance stage to "Post-Meeting Qualification". Rule 5 — Fix mislabeled meeting types (active deals)

Rule 6 — Backfill contact engagements
For every deal (including "Contact later" stage), finds linked contacts and copies their recent engagement records (last 2 weeks) as deal associations — ensuring emails and calls from contacts appear on the deal timeline even if not logged directly against it.
Rule 7 — Daily meeting type hygiene (all stages)
Fetches all meetings created today across the entire HubSpot account. For each meeting with a first-meeting type label, looks up its associated deal, fetches all meetings on that deal, and checks chronological order. Any meeting that is not the first on the deal gets relabeled to Embedded Negotiations. Covers all pipeline stages — including POC, Negotiations, and beyond — that Rule 5 does not touch. Count of corrections included in the daily Slack report.
Rule 9 — Reschedule early tasks to 10am CET
For every active-stage deal, fetches all open future tasks. Any task with a due time before 09:00 UTC (10am CET) is rescheduled to 09:00 UTC on the same day — ensuring tasks don't surface before the working day starts.

How it works

Poller (every 30 min): A background thread fetches fresh data from all connectors, upserts items into SQLite (deduped by item ID), then calls Claude to generate a structured digest. The digest is stored in the DB and served to the bot on demand.

Digest structure: Claude classifies items into 4 buckets — (1) Tasks: max 7, things Leo personally must act on (reply, decide, approve) — excludes internal Albato updates, automated notifications, OOO replies, support tickets, things addressed to other teammates; (2) Hot topics: max 4, important things happening at Albato that Leo should know but don't require action — includes deals going Lost or Contact Later; (3) Mentions: max 8, direct messages from real humans waiting for Leo's personal response — excludes anything already in tasks; (4) Meetings: next 48 hours only. Every item includes its original creation date (not fetch time).

Telegram bot: Leo interacts via Telegram. Sending "новости" / "what's new" triggers an immediate poll + returns fetch counts per source (how many items fetched, lookback window used) + today's digest. Any other message uses the DB from the last automatic poll (max 30 min stale). Bot can also take actions: send emails, create calendar events, fetch live items from a specific source with a custom lookback.

News trigger flow: On "новости", bot runs run_once_with_counts(), waits up to 120s, then appends a fetch summary to the user message before passing to Claude: sources, counts, lookback windows, and digest totals (tasks/topics/mentions/meetings).

Telethon (personal Telegram reading): Uses a StringSession stored as TELEGRAM_SESSION_STRING env var — no interactive phone auth needed on Railway. Runs in an isolated thread with its own asyncio event loop to avoid conflicts with python-telegram-bot's async context. Thread replies in Pachka are fetched inline. Slack thread replies fetched via conversations.replies for messages with reply_count > 0.

Google OAuth: credentials.json and token.json are written to /app/ on startup from GOOGLE_CREDENTIALS / GOOGLE_TOKEN env vars (raw JSON). Token auto-refreshes via refresh_token. If token expires permanently, it must be refreshed locally and GOOGLE_TOKEN updated in Railway.

Rules
🤖 Amy persona
All drafts are written in Amy Walker's voice using the tone-of-voice guide at kb/kb_tone_of_voice.md. Claude never reveals it is an AI. Tone: matches the prospect's register. Goal: establish relevance first — never push for a meeting prematurely.
📚 Context tiering
Context loaded depends on mode and intent. Draft: Haiku classifies the prospect's message as simple / neutral / complex before any context is fetched. Simple (ack, "let's chat", availability) → thread + ToV + guidelines + winning threads only; no CRM, no product KB. Neutral → adds CRM + product KB. Complex (objection, deep question, competitor) → full context. FU#1–2: thread + ToV + guidelines (early set, 8 sections) + winning threads; no CRM notes, no product KB. FU#3–4: full CRM + product KB + winning threads. Product KB: keyword-scored retrieval from kb/kb_sources.json (up to 6 chunks for draft, 4 for respond). Sources refreshed bi-weekly by kb-refresh.yml cron (Mon + Thu 06:00 UTC).
🔁 Infinite loop guard
In respond mode, if the Slack message that triggered the run was posted by the bot itself (Amy's user ID U0B9XCUHRCM or any bot_id), the agent exits immediately. Metadata is embedded in the parent message's fallback text as an HTML comment (<!--meta:{...}-->) and Slack &lt;/&gt; escapes are unescaped before parsing.
👤 Owner identification
Resolved from owner_email in the webhook payload (e.g. l.goldfarb@albato.com → Leo's inbox). Pattern-matched against SENDER_PATTERNS (goldfarb, guerci, nick.g, mike/fedorinin). Falls back to scanning the Front conversation's last outbound from-address if not provided. Each team member has a distinct Front inbox, channel, and signature ID used when creating the reply draft.
🏆 Winning threads KB
Populated three ways: (1) automatically when a HubSpot deal moves to "Meeting Booked" — learn mode extracts the Front conversation from the first inbound reply onward; (2) rep scores 8–10 in Slack — Amy fetches the lead's real email from Front + Amy's draft and saves the pair; (3) rep replies save thread. Quoted reply chains stripped from lead messages. Stored in kb/kb_winning_threads.json, deduplicated by deal_id, committed to repo.
📋 Deal context
Fetches 25+ populated deal properties via HubSpot CRM v3 API (buying_signal, needs, semaphore, decision_criteria, red_flags, embedded_competitors, arr__deal_, timeline, stakeholders, etc.) plus engagements. ICP EVALUATION and Buying Signal notes are always prioritised (up to 3), then up to 2 regular notes and 5 emails. Agent-generated notes excluded.
🛡️ Draft validator (all modes)
Every draft (inbound reply and follow-up) is checked by Sonnet 4.6 before posting to Slack. The validator checks against kb/kb_tone_of_voice.md and kb/kb_guidelines.md and returns JSON with pass, violations, and feedback. Scope constraint: validator only flags violations visible in the text itself — it does not flag context-grounded claims (conference references, prior conversation mentions) as unverifiable since it doesn't have access to the thread or CRM. On failure, feedback is injected back as a rewrite instruction — up to 3 attempts. If all 3 fail, a failure alert is posted to Slack instead (with deal name, deal ID, and violations). When a rewrite happened, the Slack message shows ⚠️ Rewritten after validation (attempt N/3) with the specific bullets.
🚫 No inferred problems
Amy must only reference problems explicitly stated in the email thread or HubSpot deal notes. She must never assume pain points based on company type, industry, or persona — even if they're plausible. Rule enforced in kb/kb_guidelines.md ("Never infer a problem the lead hasn't stated") and checked by the Sonnet validator on every draft.
📅 No premature meeting pitch
Amy must not invite the lead to a call or demo unless the lead has explicitly signalled openness to one in the thread. Even when readiness is clear, Amy validates interest before sending the booking link. Rule enforced in kb/kb_guidelines.md and checked by the Sonnet validator.
📆 Google Calendar integration
When a prospect asks about a specific day or time (detected by Haiku classifier), Amy fetches Mariana's real free slots via Google Calendar freeBusy API and offers them in the lead's timezone (EST, PST, GMT, etc. auto-detected from the message). Slots are shown at 1-hour intervals, bounded by Mariana's HubSpot booking availability windows per weekday. No timezone conversion explanation is included. If no specific day is mentioned, the HubSpot booking link is used instead.
💳 Paid campaign handling
If the deal name contains "paid" or the thread mentions an incentive, Amy may reference it in any follow-up including FU#1/2. The incentive goes in a P.S. line after the sign-off: "P.S. Still happy to offer $200 for 60 minutes of your time." If the lead already agreed to a meeting but hasn't booked, the entire email can be one sentence + closing question — no substantive opener needed. The closing question is always required.
💰 Token + cost display
Every Slack draft post (both inbound draft mode and followup mode) includes a usage line in the format N in / N out · $0.00XX calculated from the Anthropic response usage object. _format_usage_line(usage, model=) applies model-aware pricing: Sonnet 4.6 rates ($3/M input, $15/M output) for followup mode; Opus 4.8 rates ($15/M input, $75/M output) for draft mode. Expected cost: ~$0.07/follow-up (down from ~$0.41 with Opus). If validation rewrites were needed, the Slack message also shows "⚠️ Rewritten after validation (attempt N/3)".
